Field Definitions

ID
A unique identifier for the wiring leg (e.g., L-001).
Typical
The number of identical, parallel wires in a single run (e.g., 4 for the 4 strings combined in a home run).
Initial/Final Location
The start and end points of the wire run (e.g., PV Array to Junction Box).
Conductor (Gauge/Type/Material)
The properties of the main current-carrying wires. Gauge is the size (AWG), Type is the insulation (e.g., THWN-2), and Material is Copper or Aluminum.
Conduit
The nominal trade size of the conduit containing the wires (e.g., 3/4").
# of Parallel Circuits
The number of separate circuits being run together.
CCC (Current Carrying Conductors)
The total number of conductors in the conduit that generate heat (hots and neutrals). Used to calculate the Conduit Fill Factor.
Fill %
The calculated percentage of the conduit's usable area that is filled by all wires, including the ground wire.
OCPD (Overcurrent Protection Device)
The amperage rating of the circuit breaker or fuse protecting the circuit.
EGC (Equipment Grounding Conductor)
The safety ground wire. Its size (Gauge) is determined by the OCPD rating, and its material is typically bare or insulated copper.
Temp. Corr. (°C / Factor)
A derating factor applied to wire ampacity based on the ambient temperature where it is installed. The factor is calculated from the temperature based on NEC tables.
Conduit Fill Factor
A derating factor applied when more than 3 CCCs are bundled in a conduit, accounting for heat buildup. It is calculated from the CCC value.
Cont. Current (Continuous Current)
The expected, steady-state current the circuit will carry during normal operation.
Max Current
For solar circuits, this is the value used to size wires and breakers, calculated as Continuous Current x 1.25 per NEC requirements.
Base Amp
The maximum ampacity of the conductor in ideal conditions, based on its size and temperature rating, before any derating factors are applied.
Derated Amp
The final, true ampacity of the conductor after applying all correction factors (temperature and conduit fill). This value must be greater than the Max Current.
Term Temp (Terminal Temperature Rating)
The maximum temperature rating of the terminals the wire is connected to (usually 75°C for standard breakers).
Length
The one-way length of the wire run in feet.
VD % (Voltage Drop)
The percentage of voltage lost over the length of the wire due to resistance. Should typically be kept below 3%.
